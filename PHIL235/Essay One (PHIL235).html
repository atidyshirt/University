In our lives, we are living in a world of machines. We depend on machines for every field of our life. Everything around us is applications of artificial intelligence. Air conditioners, cameras, video games, medical equipment, were traffic lights, refrigerators, etc work on a “smart” technology (Essays, UK. November 2018), You don't have to look far to find headlines such as “Why the future doesn’t need us” or “Global technology regulation and potentially Apocalyptic technological Threats”. These articles are distinctly pessimistic in their view of the near future as society advances in the realm of Artificial Intelligence. In this essay, I will assess the threats of Bill Joy's GNR technologies and justify whether it is practical and safe to continue to pursue advancements in these areas. I will mainly address the advancement of the Robotics part of GNR technology (R), specifically the advancement of Artificial/Super Intelligence, and critique Bill Joy's philosophy outlined in "Why the Future Doesn't Need Us".
